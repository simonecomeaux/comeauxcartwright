Where We'll Be · Day One

Lazareti

Just outside the old city walls, where they meet the sea near the Ploče Gate, Lazareti was built in the 1600s as Dubrovnik's quarantine station: arriving sailors and merchants were held here in isolation before being allowed into the city. It's one of the best-preserved complexes of its kind anywhere in the Mediterranean: long stone halls and open courtyards built directly into the rock, looking straight out at the water.

These days it's a cultural venue instead of a checkpoint, which is where we come in, hors d'oeuvres in hand, for the first night of the weekend.

Where We'll Be · Day Three

BOWA Dubrovnik

An overwater lounge on a quiet stretch of the Adriatic, reached by boat and built right out over the water: thatched cabanas, clear turquoise shallows, and not much else on the agenda.

Entirely optional, but it's the softest possible way to close out the weekend: swim, eat something fresh off the grill, and stay as long as the sun's out.
