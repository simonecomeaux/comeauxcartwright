The Evening

We open the weekend with a welcome party and hors d'oeuvres at Lazareti, just outside the old city walls near the Ploče Gate. Built in the 1600s as Dubrovnik's quarantine station, it's one of the best-preserved complexes of its kind in the Mediterranean: long stone halls and open courtyards looking straight out at the water. These days it's a cultural venue instead of a checkpoint, which is where we come in.

An optional beach and boat day at BOWA Dubrovnik, an overwater lounge reached by boat: thatched cabanas and clear turquoise shallows. After We Say I Do

Extend the Trip

Already making the trip to Croatia? Consider staying on. Here are two routes worth taking, either one makes a great week.

Route One

The Dalmatian Coast

Dubrovnik → Makarska Riviera → Brač → Šolta → Hvar → Vis → Split

Fly into Dubrovnik and spend a couple of days getting your bearings in the old city, then head up the coast to the Makarska Riviera. From there, island-hop through Brač, Šolta, Hvar, and Vis before flying home out of Split. This is the route we did ourselves, and we loved every stop of it.

Route Two

The Balkan Loop

Dubrovnik → Bosnia & Herzegovina → Montenegro → Albania

Go the other direction: from Dubrovnik into Bosnia and Herzegovina, on to Montenegro, and, if you have the extra days, across the border into Albania, one of our favorite countries. Wilder, less traveled, and just as beautiful.
